My Teaching Philosophy and Style ✨

I have a  student-centric teaching style which  means I focus on what works best for you. Instead of just talking and having you listen, I want you to get involved and learn by doing things yourself. Here’s what it looks like:

  1. Active Learning: You do more hands-on activities, group work, and projects, not just listen to lectures.

  2. Collaboration: You work with your classmates on group projects and learn from each other.

  3. Personalized Learning: The teacher tries to match the lessons to your interests and how you learn best.

  4. Critical Thinking: You get to solve problems and think for yourself, not just memorize facts.

  5. Teacher as Guide: The teacher helps you and supports you as you explore and learn, rather than just giving you all the answers.

  6. Student Choice: You get some say in what and how you learn, making it more interesting and relevant to you.

  7. Real-World Connections: Lessons relate to real-life situations, so you see why what you're learning matters.

  8. Ongoing Feedback: You get regular feedback on how you're doing and can reflect on your progress, not just take tests.
