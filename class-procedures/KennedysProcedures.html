Kennedy's Procedures & Expectations

Procedures are the step-by-step instructions on how to do things. When procedures are not followed, procedures are reviewed and retaught.

Expectations describe the behaviors we want to see used. When expectations are not met, the expected behavior is reinforced.  

School Rule Alert  Failure to meet these procedures and expectations will result in disciplinary actions.

Safety Procedures

Procedure: 

  1. Stay Calm!
  2. Gather your keys, wallet, water, phone (basically your stuff).
  3. Push your chair in then exit the classroom safely.  
  4. Safely walk out to the parking area.  
  5. Line up in rows exactly the same as the class seating chart.

Procedure: 

  1. Stay Calm!
  2. Gather your keys, wallet, water, phone (basically your stuff).  
  3. Make sure your desk is cleared (no signs of students in this room!).  
  4. Go to the designated safe area and sit down.
  5. Turn your phone/watch off or on silent and do not use your phone for any reason.
  6. Remain as silent as possible

Procedure: 

  1. Bandaids are in the classroom.  
  2. If you are suddenly ill, vomit in the trash cans.  
  3. Notify me immediately if you get blood, vomit, or other bodily fluids on anything other than yourself.  

Procedure: 

  1. Be in uniform with your badge showing at all time on campus
  2. Report suspicious person and items to a faculty or staff member immediately

Classroom Procedures & Expectations

Cellphones

School Rule Alert

Procedure:  Turn your phone on silent and stow your phone in your bag.

Get permission to use it in class.

Use a hall pass to access your phone.

There is a School Rule which requires you to engage work which is related to your course objectives during instructional time.

Expectations  I never see or hear your phone unless you have my permission to use it in class

 

Dress Code

School Rule Alert

Procedure: Be in uniform before entering the classroom.

 

Hall Passes

Procedure:   Hall passes are on my desk.  Fill out the hall pass down to the teacher's signature. Bring the pass to me for a signature and a time. You must be specific about where you are going then go only there and immediately back again. Do not be gone longer than 10 minutes on a hall pass.

Procedure:   In the event of a fire alarm, go immediately to our meet up area.  In the event of a lockdown, shelter in place.  Lock the bathroom door or stall door and perch on the seat so that your feet cannot be seen under the door.

There is a School Rule which requires you to engage in work which is related to your course objectives during instructional time.  

    

Entering the class

Procedure: Be in uniform and do not disrupt others.  Go to your assigned seat.

School Rule Alert : If you are late, you must have a late pass or a late bus pass.

Expectation: If you are entering the classroom late and the Pledge of Allegiance is underway, please stand just inside the door.

Start of class

Procedure: 

  1. Be in uniform and in the classroom before the music ends.
  2. Stand during music - Movement and visiting time!
  3. End of Music - Whoop in
  4. Stand behind your (pushed in chair), silently, with nothing in your hands during announcements
  5. Moment of Silence:  Remain standing and silent during this time.
  6. Pledge: direct your attention towards the flag
  7. After pledge, rotate your chair to face the teacher for any additional announcements before the start of the day.

Expectation:  I expect you to remain standing from the moment the music starts until the end of the Pledge.  I expect you to be silent, still, nothing in your hands, and not interacting with others or with objects through the announcements, the moment of silence, and the Pledge.  I expect your silent attention for a few minutes following this.

      

End of class

Procedure: My end of class signal is “That is our 2 minute mark.”  When I have said this:

  • Please log out of your accounts and "update and turn off" the computer
  • Return any borrowed supplies
  • Throw away your trash.
  • Stack the markerboards.
  • Put supplies back in the basket.
  • Gather your belongings
  • Push your chairs in and stand behind your chair – this will be your signal to me that you are ready to be dismissed
  • Please remain at your desk and wait for the exit tradition (not the bell)
  • The exit tradition is to whoop out.

Expectations: I expect you to remain engaged in your work until I call time.  I expect you to stay at your assigned seat (not at the door) until the class is whooped out.

  

Attention Signal

Expectation: Stop working, rotate your chair to face me, politely help other people around you to notice that I want their attention.  

 

You and Your Desk

Expectation:

  1. Do remain in your seat.
  2. Do keep your area tidy.
  3. Do keep your head in an upright position and your eyes open.
  4. Do sit in the standard manner- back against the chair back, two feet on the floor.
  5. There is a School Rule which requires you to engage in work which is related to your course objectives during instructional time.
  6. Do not have food or drink at your computer or on the floor or anywhere else in the classroom.
  7. Do not tip your chair.
  8. Do not horseplay.
  9. Do not touch other people's things or their computers.
  10. Do not wander around the class, be otherwise off task, and/or interfering with others concentrating on their work.

        

Late to class

Procedure:  Get a late slip or a tardy bus slip before coming to class

IF the pledge is in progress when you arrive at class, stop inside the door and direct your attention to the flag.

 

Headphones

Expectation: Yes.

Use your own or borrow a class set.  Adjust the volume so that the person right next to you is not disturbed by any sound coming from your headset.  You may not have any movies or videos showing on your desktop, not even super tiny reduced sized.  There is a School Rule which requires you to engage in work which is related to your course objectives during instructional time.

Getting Supplies

Procedure: It is always okay to get these things for yourself without permission however they may be hard to locate.

Walking in the hall

Procedure:  Observe professional behavior. Walk calmly and quietly to your destination. Be on your best behavior.  Do not talk in the halls.  Do not run, yell, horseplay, jump, stare in windows, knock on walls or windows, or engage in other behaviors which embarrass you and me. Obey instructions from other faculty and staff.

Working with others

Procedure: Highly encouraged.  Seek permission before changing seats and do not change seats during the start of class procedures or you might be marked absent.  Remember you can collaborate using platforms such as GitHub and GoogleDocs.

Expectations:  Please keep your volume down as if you were working in a library. Please follow the professionalism skills when working with others.

   

Language

Expectations:  Please use language that is appropriate for a professional work environment.
